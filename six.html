How Money is Made - Interactive Factory

Click through the steps to learn how money is made!

Step 1: Collecting Raw Materials

To create money, we need raw materials. Coins are made from metals like copper, nickel, and zinc, while banknotes are printed on a special blend of cotton and linen. These materials are carefully chosen to ensure durability and security.

Step 2: Minting Coins

Once the raw metal is prepared, it is melted and rolled into thin sheets. These sheets are then cut into small circular blanks, which are later stamped with designs and values to transform them into official coins.

Step 3: Printing Notes

Banknotes are created using high-security printing presses. They are designed with unique patterns, colors, and special inks that make them hard to counterfeit. Each note is printed with advanced techniques to include security features like watermarks and holograms.

Step 4: Quality Check

Every coin and banknote undergoes strict quality control checks. Machines and experts inspect them to ensure there are no defects or errors. This step is crucial in preventing fake money from entering circulation.

Step 5: Distribution

Once the money has passed all quality checks, it is packaged and sent to banks. From there, it reaches ATMs and bank counters, where people can withdraw and use it for everyday transactions.
